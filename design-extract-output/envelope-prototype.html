Brooklyn, 2024
Dear friend,

The kitchen had been someone else's before it was mine. I knew this because the tile behind the stove carried three different layers of grout, each a slightly different shade of white, each applied by a hand I would never meet.

These twenty-five photographs were made over three years in kitchens, stairwells, and borrowed rooms across Brooklyn and Beirut. Small domestic rituals that repeat across distance and never quite translate.

I made them into prints because some photographs want to be held, not scrolled past. Each one is archival pigment on cotton rag, in an edition of ten.

With care,
Thalia
